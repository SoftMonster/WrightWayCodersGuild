🏠 Wallpapering a landing is a bit of a pro-level task: tight turns 🔄, stairwells 🪜, mixed light 💡, and often awkward heights 📏. Below are tricks of the trade decorators use to balance time ⏱️, quality ⭐, and effectiveness ✅, with specific advice for landings.

1️⃣ Planning & Prep (where pros save the most time)

📐 Measure like a pro

  • 📏 Measure the full drop from skirting to ceiling at the highest point (stairs often rise into the landing).
  • ➕ Add 100–150 mm extra per drop for trimming ✂️ and pattern matching.
  • 🧮 Count drops wall by wall — don’t just rely on room perimeter maths for landings.

⏱️ Time tip: Draw a quick wall sketch ✏️ and number each drop in order of hanging.

🧻 Choose the right wallpaper

  • 🪣 Paste-the-wall wallpapers save huge time and reduce mess on stairs.
  • 🔁 Non-woven wallpapers are more forgiving for repositioning.

🚫 Avoid:

  • 🌑 Very dark colours (show every seam)
  • 🔶 Large bold repeats (wasteful and slow)
  • 🌾 Grasscloth or natural fibres (hard mode)

📏 Trade rule: Landing = medium pattern, subtle texture, forgiving repeat.

🧱 Surface prep is non-negotiable

  • 🛠️ Fill everything — landings are hit by side light from windows.
  • 🪵 Sand smooth and seal bare plaster with diluted paste or primer.
  • 🔌 Remove light switches, smoke alarms, and handrail brackets if possible.

⭐ Quality rule: Wallpaper never hides bad walls — it magnifies them.

2️⃣ Tools Professionals Rely On 🧰

🔧 Essential pro tools

  • 🖌️ 9" roller + tray (for paste-the-wall)
  • 📏 Wide filling knife (as a cutting guide)
  • 🔪 Snap-off knife (new blade every 1–2 drops)
  • 🧻 Seam roller (light pressure only)
  • 📐 Plumb line or laser level
  • 🖌️ Paste brush for edges
  • 🪜 Step ladder and stair ladder / platform

⏱️ Time tip: A laser level is faster than a plumb line and pays for itself.

3️⃣ Order of Work (this matters on a landing)

👀 Always start at the most visible wall

Usually:

  • ⬆️ Facing you as you come up the stairs
  • 🪟 Or opposite the main window

➡️ Work away from it so any small mismatches end up in corners.

📐 Don’t wrap corners

  • ↔️ Stop 5–10 mm short of internal corners.
  • 📏 Start the next drop fresh and plumb.

⚠️ Trade rule: Wallpaper doesn’t like corners — never force it.

⬇️ Work top-down on stairwells

  • 🏗️ Hang full-length drops from the landing ceiling down.
  • ✂️ Trim excess at stair angle after smoothing.

🦺 Safety tip: Never overreach — move the platform.

4️⃣ Hanging Techniques That Separate DIY from Pro

🧷 Use a “slip” method

  • 📌 Lightly tack the top
  • ⬇️ Let the rest hang loose
  • 🖐️ Smooth from the centre out, working down

This avoids stretch ↔️ and pattern creep.

🧩 Pattern matching tricks

  • 👁️ Match at eye level first, not the ceiling.
  • ⬆️ Slight mismatches at the top are less noticeable.

🎩 Pro cheat: If the pattern drifts, gently lift and re-seat — don’t stretch.

🧵 Seams: less is more

  • ❌ Don’t overlap
  • 🚫 Don’t over-roll seams
  • 🧽 Wipe paste immediately with a barely damp sponge

⭐ Quality rule: Most seam failures come from too much pressure.

5️⃣ Dealing with Awkward Features

📐 Stair angles

  • ⬇️ Hang full drop straight
  • 🖐️ Smooth fully
  • ✂️ Cut angle last using a wide knife as a guide

🚫 Never pre-cut.

🔌 Light switches & sockets

  • ⬇️ Hang straight over them
  • ➕ Small cross-cut
  • ✂️ Trim once paper is flat

⚡ Cleaner and faster than cutting shapes beforehand.

🪜 Handrails

  • ✅ Best: remove them
  • If not:
    • ✂️ Cut neatly around brackets
    • 📍 Keep joints away from them if possible

6️⃣ Drying & Finishing (often overlooked)

🌬️ Control drying

  • 🔥 No strong heat or open windows
  • ⚖️ Even drying prevents seams opening

🔍 Final inspection

  • 👀 Check seams from multiple angles
  • ⏱️ Re-smooth bubbles within first 20 minutes
  • 🖌️ Touch edges with paste brush if needed

8️⃣ Common Pro Mistakes (to avoid) 🚫

  • 📏 Starting without a plumb reference
  • ⏩ Rushing corners
  • ↔️ Stretching paper to force alignment
  • 💡 Ignoring light direction
  • 🔪 Using blunt blades

🏆 Pro Rule of Thumb

90% of wallpapering success on a landing is prep, access, and order — not skill.
